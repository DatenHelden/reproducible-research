Creating a Central Knowledge Hub for Reproducible Research with R

Reproducibility = Transparency (open data, models & software) + Automatisation

View project on GitHub

The Idea

Wrapping up useR2015 in a few words: it was a fantastic conference. We met a lot of wonderful open minded people, who enlarged our water research driven universe.

All R users are taking advantage of the nearly infinite problems that can be solved with it in a systematic, transparent and automatised way. Both, automatisation and transparency are a prerequisite for achieving the "gold standard": reproducible research.

The Challenge

Creating a centralized R knowledge hub for reproducible research for wrapping the state of the art tools & packages for R enabling this challenge.

What to do?

  1. Screen the R world for already available tools which you are using because of their excellant functionality.

  2. Add them

  3. Explain from a functional point of view why your proposed tool is so great

  4. Community should evaluate the usefulness!

Which components?

What are the best tools/packages for:

1. Data

  • Import: getting different file formats, DBs & Web data into the R universe
  • Preparation: cleaning, aggregating
  • Analysis: graphical visualisation

2. Modelling

Examples:

  • Wrap your model

    • Machine learning: mlr package
    • Environmental: e.g. openmodelling

  • do you have some more?

Usual workflow:

  • Parameterise
  • Run,
  • Analyse,
  • Calibrate,
  • Validate,
  • Predict and .....

3. Document

your results (automatically).

4. Deployment:

  • Web-based or stand-alone,
  • Development or production,
  • in case of production: does the used tool scale without being too expensive?

5. Additional topics:

  • Crowdsourcing: e.g. Ohmage

  • Learning:

    • OpenBooks: e.g. statistics OpenIntro, ....
    • Training: DataCamp

Missed some? Please help us!!!

Who?

Every member of the R community is invited to contribute!

When?

Quality of the recommendations should always be the major objective.
